About Me



I am a PhD candidate in the School of Public Health and Health Systems, at the University of Waterloo. I am interested in using knowledge translation strategies to improve nutrition care in healthcare institutions, and how staff can work together to change practice. I have a keen interest in knowledge translation and implementation science, with an overall focus on converting research into practice.




Research Interests

Implementing Nutrition Care Practices

I am a core member of the More-2-Eat (M2E) Phase 1 and 2 implementation projects, led by Professor Heather Keller. In Phase 1, we used participatory action research to improve nutrition care in 5 hospitals in 4 provinces in Canada. In Phase 2, we identified the most important components from Phase 1 for changing practice (a champion, engaged staff, local data, and communication with other hospitals), and designed a project that aims to sustain and scale up the Phase 1 success (mainly nutrition screening and improved triaging of at risk patients). My PhD thesis used interviews, focus groups and a questionnaire to explore staff views on nutrition care in their hospital unit, how to change practices and ensure that the effective changes are sustained. http://m2e.nutritioncareincanada.ca/

International Nutrition Education and Implementation

I am an international core team member of the UK Need for Nutrition Education/Innovation Programme (NNEdPro).  NNEdPro is an award-winning, international and interdisciplinary network based in Cambridge, England. NNEdPro aims to advance and implement nutrition knowledge to improve health, wellbeing and society. I am co-lead of the NNEdPro Global Innovation Panel (GIP), which promotes international initiatives and knowledge exchange in nutrition, relevant to clinical and public health practice for medical and health professionals. Through NNEdPro, I am a Visiting Scholar at Wolfson College, University of Cambridge.


Key Publications:

 

Proceedings of the Inaugural International Summit for Medical Nutrition Education and Research. Public Health 2016.


Setting Priorities for Research in Medical Nutrition Education: An International Approach. BMJ Open 2016.



Ongoing Projects 

I work with several community/primary care groups, including Family Health Teams, to encourage and support implementation of nutrition screening. I am also working with data from the Canadian Longitudinal Study on Aging (CLSA) to further examine the validity of a screening tool. I am involved with the Canadian Malnutrition Task Force, and am a member of the Canadian Patient Safety Institute Knowledge Translation and Implementation Science Faculty. In 2016, I was an Interdisciplinary Fellow with the Canadian Frailty Network.
